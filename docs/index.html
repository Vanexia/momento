Momento.

A local Windows game recorder with a built-in clip editor. Everything stays on your machine.

Windows 10/11 NVIDIA GPU (NVENC) No account required No telemetry

What it does

Momento sits quietly in your system tray. When it detects a game launching, it starts capturing the game's window via Windows Graphics Capture along with your microphone and the system's audio output. When the game closes, recording stops automatically and the resulting MKV file lands in your chosen folder.

The built-in editor lets you browse past recordings, trim clips, drop bookmarks during gameplay with a hotkey, and export final cuts as MP4. There's nothing to configure to get started — Momento picks sensible defaults for resolution, framerate, and quality.

Features

Auto-record on game launch

Detects ~650 popular PC games out of the box. Add your own; toggle individual games on or off.

In-app clip editor

Timeline with trim handles, zoom, bookmark ticks, time inputs. Preview playback before export.

Bookmark hotkey

Press F8 mid-game to drop a marker. Find the highlight instantly when editing afterwards.

Crash-safe MKV

Cluster-based muxing means a hard crash loses at most one second. Manual repair tool for the rest.

Quality presets

Low/Medium/High or custom bitrate. NVENC hardware encoding so recording doesn't tax your CPU.

YouTube upload (opt-in)

Right-click any clip → Upload to YouTube. Title, description, privacy in-app. Optional, never automatic.

How Momento handles your data

Local-first by design

  • No telemetry. No analytics. No usage tracking.
  • No account required to use Momento.
  • Recordings are stored only on your machine, in a folder you choose.
  • No background network activity. Momento makes no outbound calls until you explicitly upload.

The one exception: YouTube upload.

If you opt in by connecting a YouTube account and clicking "Upload to YouTube" on a clip, that single upload goes to YouTube via Google's standard API. Sign-in happens in your browser on Google's site — Momento never sees your password. The sign-in token is encrypted on disk via Windows DPAPI, bound to your Windows user account. System requirements

Windows 10 (1903 or newer) or Windows 11.

NVIDIA GPU with NVENC support (any GTX 10-series or newer, all RTX cards). NVENC handles the video encode so Momento is light on CPU during recording.

Approximately 5 GB free disk for the app and a comfortable buffer for recordings (recordings are large — plan for ~1-3 GB per hour at the default High quality preset).
